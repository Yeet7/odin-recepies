Tiramisu

Description:

Tiramisu is a classic Italian dessert that combines layers of coffee-soaked ladyfingers and a creamy mixture of mascarpone cheese, whipped cream, and eggs. Here's a recipe that you can follow to make this delicious treat at home.

Ingredients:

  • 6 large egg yolks
  • 1 cup dark rum
  • 3 ounces mascarpone cheese
  • 1 cup heavy whipping cream
  • 1 cup boiling water
  • 1 tablespoon instant espresso powder
  • 1 package ladyfingers
  • Cocoa powder for dusting top (as needed)

Steps:

  1. Lightly oil an 8x8-inch baking dish.
  2. Set a large heatproof bowl over a pan of simmering water to create a double boiler. Whisk together the egg yolks and 3/4 cup of the sugar in the bowl until the sugar has dissolved, the mixture has increased slightly in volume, and it looks light yellow. Rub a little of the mixture between two fingers to ensure it feels smooth and silky.
  3. Gradually whisk in 2 ounces (4 tablespoons) of the rum and continue whisking rapidly over the double boiler for about 10 minutes, until the mixture is thick, foamy, and very pale yellow. Remove the bowl from the double boiler and set aside until cooled to at least 90°F or room temperature.
  4. Beat the mascarpone on medium speed for 15 to 30 seconds, until it smooths out and softens. Gradually add the cream in a thin, slow stream until it's all incorporated. Continue beating with the mixer on medium-high speed for another 30 to 60 seconds, until the mixture holds firm, not-quite-stiff peaks.
  5. Fold 1/3 of the whipped mascarpone-cream mixture into the zabaglione to lighten it, then fold in the remaining 2/3.
  6. In a wide, shallow dish, whisk the remaining 1/4 cup sugar with the remaining 1 ounce (2 tablespoons) of rum, the boiling water, and the espresso powder. Stir to dissolve the sugar and espresso.
  7. Dunk one ladyfinger at a time in the espresso liquid, turning it so that all sides are evenly moistened — a quick dunk is all you need; any more and the ladyfingers start to disintegrate. Arrange the dunked ladyfingers in rows on the bottom of the baking dish. Once you finish the layer, spread 1/2 of the mascarpone mixture over the top. Repeat dunking and arranging a second layer, and spread the remaining mascarpone over top.
  8. Cover the tiramisu and refrigerate for at least 8 hours or up to 24 hours to give the tiramisu time to firm up and for the ladyfingers to soften.
  9. Dust with cocoa before serving. Serve in wedges directly from the pan. Leftovers will keep for about 5 days.
